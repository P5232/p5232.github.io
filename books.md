02/2023: <u>The Power of Fun: How to Feel Alive Again</u> by Catherine Price
I am currently reading this book as a recommendation from Meg. There are actually some interesting connections between this and <u>Man's Search for Meaning</u> if you look close enough. But the writing style and content could not be further from each other. 

01/2023: <u>Man's Search for Meaning</u> by Viktor E. Frankl.
I read this in print after listening to an audio version. There are too many lessons and quotes to take away from this book that it would be an injustice to pick out my favorites here. The title of this book captures it well. If you are ever going through a phase of wondering what meaning there is in life, read this book again.

12/2022: <u>The Mare</u> by Mary Gaitskill.
After reading <u>This is Pleasure</u>, I thought this would be as interesting a read. It had some good characters and some good themes, but it didn't have that spark that made me want to pick it back up and read more. I did not finish.

11/2022: <u>This is Pleasure</u> by Mary Gaitskill.
A short story narrated by two alternating characters that reveal the complicated interactions between men and women in work and life. This book was a welcomed distraction from the non-fiction I typically read.
